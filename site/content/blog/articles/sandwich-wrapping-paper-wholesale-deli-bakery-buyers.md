## Introduction

What should buyers check before ordering sandwich wrapping paper wholesale? The direct answer is: define the food profile, service route, wrap style, grease and moisture exposure, print requirement, document scope and packing method before asking for a bulk price. Then approve production-representative samples with real sandwiches, bakery items or takeaway foods under the actual holding and display conditions.

Sandwich wrapping paper looks simple, but it has to solve several practical problems at once. It touches food, controls oil and moisture appearance, folds quickly at the counter, supports brand presentation, separates cleanly from the pack and gives importers a document trail for food-contact review.

This guide is written for foodservice packaging distributors, deli and bakery chains, supermarket prepared-food teams, caterers, importers and private-label buyers comparing deli wrap paper bulk options. It focuses on buyer decisions: how to specify sandwich wrap, how to test samples, how to review food-contact evidence, how to control custom printing and how to inspect shipments without relying on vague "food grade" claims.

## 1. Start With the Food and Service Route

A sandwich wrap specification should start with how the paper will actually be used. Dry bakery sandwiches, oily breakfast wraps, toasted panini, chilled deli subs, sauced flatbreads and pastry counter items do not expose the paper to the same combination of fat, moisture, heat, acidity and handling time.

Define these points before requesting samples:

- food type, such as chilled sandwich, hot panini, wrap, croissant sandwich, pastry, burger-style item, fried snack or bakery liner;
- food condition, including dry, oily, sauced, acidic, chilled, warm, hot filled or steam-heavy;
- service route, such as dine-in counter, takeaway, delivery, catering, supermarket display or airline and event service;
- expected holding time before the consumer opens the wrap;
- wrap style, including full wrap, half wrap, deli sheet, basket liner, tray liner, interleaving sheet or sleeve insert;
- sheet size, fold method and whether staff need one-hand separation at the counter;
- print side, ink coverage and whether the printed side may touch food;
- destination market and customer document requirements.

This first step prevents a common sourcing mistake: treating sandwich wrapping paper as a generic sheet purchased only by size and grammage. A sheet that works for a chilled baguette may fail around a hot toasted sandwich with cheese and sauce. ## 2. Build a Quote-Ready Specification

Wholesale quotes become comparable only when suppliers quote against the same specification. A useful sandwich wrapping paper RFQ should describe the finished article, not just the product name.

| Specification field | What to define | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Sheet dimensions | Width, length, tolerance and fold direction | Controls wrap coverage, counter speed and waste |
| Material construction | Paper color, grammage range, surface function and supplier code | Affects feel, opacity, folding and grease appearance |
| Food exposure | Fat, moisture, sauce, acidity, heat and holding time | Drives sample testing and document relevance |
| Grease and moisture target | Buyer application test or required test basis | Reduces oil marks, tearing and presentation complaints |
| Printing | Artwork version, colors, ink coverage, print side and repeat | Controls brand appearance, odour and set-off risk |
| Pack format | Inner count, sleeve or dispenser, case count, carton and pallet | Supports warehouse, counter and replenishment needs |
| Documents | Market-specific food-contact evidence tied to the finished article | Helps importer and customer review |
| Inspection | Dimensions, count, print, odour, carton marks and lot link | Confirms bulk goods match the approved sample |

Do not use grammage as the only quality indicator. Heavier paper can feel more substantial, but grease resistance, fibre structure, surface treatment, printing and the food profile all affect real performance. ## 3. Match Grease Resistance to the Sandwich Job

Buyers often ask for "greaseproof sandwich paper," but the correct performance target depends on the food. A chilled salad sandwich, a croissant with butter, a breakfast wrap with egg and sausage, and a hot toasted panini each create different risks.

A practical sample test should include:

1. Label each sample by supplier, material code, sheet size, color, print status and pack position.
2. Wrap the buyer's real product at the planned serving temperature.
3. Include high-risk fillings such as cheese, sauce, fried protein, mayonnaise, tomato, pickles or oily vegetables.
4. Hold the wrapped item for the target counter, delivery or chilled-display time.
5. Check oil staining, wet marks, tearing, sticking, odour, ink rub, set-off and hand feel.
6. Open the wrap as a consumer would and review presentation.
7. Photograph accepted and rejected samples with time, temperature and food notes.
8. Retain approved samples for the repeat-order file.

Test the most difficult item in the range, not only the easiest sandwich. If one sheet will be used as both a basket liner and a full wrap, test both modes because full wraps face folds, pressure, steam and customer handling.

## 4. Separate Food-Contact Review From Performance Testing

Grease resistance and food-contact suitability are related purchasing questions, but they are not the same. Grease testing asks whether the sheet controls oil and moisture in the buyer's application. Food-contact review asks whether the finished article and its components are suitable for the intended food-contact use in the target market.

For EU-facing projects, the European Commission explains that food-contact materials are covered by Regulation (EC) No 1935/2004, while EU food-contact legislation also identifies Regulation (EC) No 2023/2006 for good manufacturing practice. Buyers should expect evidence to match the finished sandwich wrapping paper, not just the base paper name.

For U.S.-facing projects, FDA's food-contact framework is component-based. FDA guidance on determining regulatory status explains that the overall status of a food-contact material depends on each substance that comprises the article and on the conditions of use. FDA's conditions-of-use table also separates refrigerated storage, frozen storage, hot filled or pasteurized use, and cooking or reheating conditions. A chilled deli sheet and a hot panini wrap should not be reviewed as identical uses.

Ask suppliers whether the document file identifies:

- finished article or exact material code;
- plain, colored or printed status;
- paper, treatment, coating and ink where relevant;
- intended food type and food-contact side;
- time and temperature conditions;
- destination-market framework;
- report date, issuing lab, sample identity and test scope;
- link between the document sample and the quoted SKU.

Avoid broad claims such as "FDA approved paper," "EU certified" or "100% compliant" unless the supplier can explain the exact regulation, component, declaration or test behind the wording. Stronger procurement files use precise language and traceable sample IDs.

## 5. Control Custom Printing Before Bulk Production

Custom printed sandwich wrap paper is a visible brand asset for deli counters, cafes, bakeries and takeaway chains. It can also create avoidable risk if the artwork, ink coverage, print side and sensory review are not controlled early.

Buyers should lock:

- approved artwork file, color references and revision date;
- repeat pattern direction and cutting position;
- food-contact side and print side;
- maximum ink coverage for heavy background designs;
- odour review after printing and packing;
- rub and set-off checks under compression;
- condensation or steam exposure if the product is wrapped warm;
- carton labels, SKU marks and customer item codes.

Do not approve a plain sheet and assume the printed SKU will behave the same. Printing can affect odour review, appearance, rub risk and document scope. ## 6. Choose Packing That Works at the Counter

Sandwich wrapping paper must be easy for operators to use. If sheets stick together, the inner pack collapses, the count is inconsistent or cartons are too weak for distribution, the paper can slow service even when the material itself is acceptable.

Specify the packaging hierarchy:

- sheets per inner pack;
- flat, folded, interleaved or dispenser-ready format;
- sleeve, bag, box or kraft wrap;
- inner label, lot code and customer SKU;
- master carton count, size, gross weight and compression target;
- pallet pattern and moisture protection;
- barcode, GTIN or customer item-code requirements;
- mixed-size, mixed-color or mixed-design rules if relevant.

GS1 explains that a Global Trade Item Number identifies trade items that are priced, ordered or invoiced. For distributor and retail programs, confirm which level needs identification: selling unit, inner pack, case or pallet. If the pack count, dimensions, artwork, case configuration or customer-facing claim changes, review whether the buyer's item master data and barcode handling also need an update.

## 7. Be Careful With Environmental Claims

Many buyers want sandwich wrapping paper that supports lower-plastic packaging goals, but claims must be handled carefully. A paper sheet should not be marketed as recyclable, compostable, biodegradable, PFAS-free, plastic-free or sustainable unless the finished product, destination market, disposal route and available evidence support the exact claim.

The U.S. Federal Trade Commission's Green Guides address topics such as general environmental benefit claims, compostable claims, degradable claims and recyclable claims. For EU-facing packaging, the EU Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation was published in early 2025 and updates packaging rules around reduction, recyclability and related requirements over phased timelines.

For procurement, keep environmental language inside the evidence:

- ask what claim is being made and for which market;
- confirm whether the claim applies to the finished printed sheet or only base paper;
- review test reports, declarations and disposal assumptions;
- check whether local collection or composting infrastructure is relevant;
- avoid creating stronger marketing wording than the supplier evidence supports.

This is especially important for private-label and retail projects where packaging claims may appear on a shelf-ready pack, product page or distributor catalog.

## 8. Compare Suppliers With a Practical Scorecard

Price should be compared beside application fit, document quality and supply repeatability. Use one scorecard for every supplier so purchasing, QA and brand teams evaluate the same risks.

| Evaluation area | Evidence to request | Warning sign |
|---|---|---|
| Application fit | Real-food wrap test with buyer menu items | Approval based only on catalogue photos |
| Grease and moisture control | Timed sample test plus relevant performance evidence | No link between material code and food profile |
| Printing | Artwork proof, print-side control, odour and rub review | Heavy ink coverage approved without sensory test |
| Food-contact file | Finished-article evidence for the target market | Generic "food grade" wording with no scope |
| Environmental claims | Claim-specific evidence and market fit | Broad sustainable, compostable or recyclable claims |
| Packing | Inner count, dispenser or sleeve, carton and pallet specification | Pack format decided after price approval |
| Inspection | Dimension, count, print, odour, carton and lot checks | Shipment released by quantity only |
| Change control | Notice before paper, treatment, ink, supplier or packing changes | Supplier can change material without reapproval |

A supplier with a slightly higher unit price may be stronger if the sample file is complete, the carton pack matches your warehouse system and the document evidence is easy to review.

## 9. Inspect the Shipment Against the Approved Sample

Pre-shipment inspection cannot replace food-contact review or application testing, but it can catch practical defects before goods enter the buyer's supply chain. Inspect production goods against the approved sample, purchase specification and packing file.

Inspection points include:

- purchase order, SKU, artwork revision and destination market;
- sheet width, length, tolerance, squareness and cut quality;
- paper color, stiffness, surface consistency and visible defects;
- print registration, color, rub, set-off and abnormal odour;
- greaseproof appearance compared with retained samples;
- sheets per pack, pack closure, labels and lot codes;
- carton strength, quantity, marks, dimensions and gross weight;
- pallet pattern, moisture protection and export packing;
- document revision and sample identity link.

Sampling and acceptance rules should match the buyer's risk, shipment size, channel and product history. Avoid fixed universal AQL promises. New printed SKUs and first shipments usually deserve closer review than stable repeat orders with a retained-sample history.

## FAQ

### What is sandwich wrapping paper used for?

Sandwich wrapping paper is used for deli sandwiches, bakery items, wraps, panini, takeaway foods, basket liners, tray liners and interleaving. The correct paper depends on food temperature, fat, moisture, sauce, holding time and service route.

### Is deli wrap paper the same as greaseproof paper?

Not always. Some deli wrap paper is designed mainly for handling and presentation, while greaseproof paper is selected for oil and fat resistance. Buyers should specify the intended food and test the exact finished sheet.

### Can sandwich wrapping paper be custom printed?

Yes, but printed samples should be approved as finished articles. Check artwork version, ink coverage, print side, odour, rub, set-off, color tolerance and document scope before bulk production.

### What documents should importers request?

Request food-contact evidence tied to the finished paper, including material code, print status, intended food type, food-contact side and time-temperature conditions. Also request packing specifications, lot traceability and retained-sample rules.

### Does greaseproof sandwich paper work for hot foods?

It depends on the sheet construction and the food. Hot sandwiches can add steam, fat and compression. Buyers should test production-representative samples with the real hot food and holding time before approval.

### Can sandwich wrapping paper be called compostable or recyclable?

Only if the finished article, destination market, disposal route and evidence support the exact claim. Printing, coatings, food residue and local collection systems can affect claim wording.

### How should buyers inspect bulk deli wrap paper?

Inspect sheet dimensions, paper appearance, print quality, odour, pack count, labels, carton marks, lot codes, pallet condition and document revision against the approved sample and purchase specification.
