## Introduction

When sourcing greaseproof paper sheets wholesale, start with the food and service process, not a catalogue weight or a generic “food-grade” claim. Define whether the sheet will wrap a warm burger, line a fry basket, separate pastries, protect a bakery tray or carry a custom print. Then specify contact time, food temperature, fat and moisture exposure, sheet size, fold pattern, paper construction, print, pack format and destination-market evidence. Approve the result with the real food before comparing landed prices.

Two sheets with the same dimensions and basis weight can still behave differently. Fibre structure, surface treatment, moisture and print coverage can affect grease holdout, folding, tear behaviour and odour. A pastry liner may not suit a hot, sauced sandwich, and wrapping paper is not automatically suitable for oven use.

This guide helps importers, foodservice distributors, bakery suppliers, restaurant groups and private-label teams turn an application into a quote-ready specification. It covers sample testing, document review, retail and foodservice packaging, shipment inspection and the questions that separate a useful supplier proposal from an incomplete price.

## 1. Define the Food-Contact Job First

A supplier cannot select a defensible paper from the product name alone. “Greaseproof sheet” may describe a tray liner, wrap, separator or presentation sheet, each with different stresses. Build a short intended-use statement before requesting samples.

Record whether the food is dry, oily, moist or sauced, plus contact temperature and duration. Describe whether staff will fold the sheet, place it beneath food or use it as an interleaf. State any foreseeable reheating or misuse that should inform instructions and risk review.

The U.S. FDA separates food types and conditions of use because authorization depends on the actual application. Its reference tables distinguish, among other categories, moist bakery products with or without free fat, dry solids with or without free fat, refrigerated or frozen storage, and cooking conditions. Buyers should use this logic to define the use case rather than treating an “FDA” statement as universal approval. See the FDA’s [food types and conditions of use](https://www.fda.gov/food/packaging-food-contact-substances-fcs/food-types-conditions-use-food-contact-substances).

### Common applications and the test they need

| Application | Main purchasing risk | Representative approval check |
|---|---|---|
| Burger or sandwich wrap | Oil, sauce and steam weaken or stain the sheet | Wrap the actual product for the expected holding and delivery time |
| Basket or tray liner | Grease transfers to the tray or print looks poor when wet | Hold the hottest, oiliest menu item on the liner and inspect both sides |
| Pastry or confectionery interleaf | Sticking, odour or surface marking | Stack the actual product through its normal storage period |
| Bakery counter sheet | Crumbs, fat spots and weak pickup handling | Test with staff using the normal tongs, folds and serving sequence |
| Private-label retail pack | Count, dispensing, barcode and presentation errors | Run a pack-out, shelf and scan check using production-style packaging |

If a buyer needs a sheet for baking, microwave use, freezing followed by reheating or another thermal process, say so explicitly. Do not extend a room-temperature wrapping approval to a cooking application. ## 2. Specify the Paper, Not Just the GSM

Basis weight is useful, but it does not fully define performance. A wholesale specification should identify the paper construction or grade offered, nominal basis weight and tolerance, colour, caliper if critical, surface finish, grain direction when folding or converting depends on it, and the mechanism used to achieve grease resistance. Ask the supplier to disclose whether the article is an uncoated dense paper, treated paper, coated structure or laminate, without assuming those categories are interchangeable.

The right construction depends on the application. Dry pastry separation and a hot delivery wrap may need different barrier and strength profiles. More paper is not automatically better: excess stiffness can hinder wrapping or dispensing and increase pack volume.

Avoid unsupported environmental shortcuts. “Paper,” “plastic-free,” “recyclable,” “biodegradable” and “compostable” are different claims. Printing, coatings, food residue, collection systems and local acceptance can change the correct disposal route. Require evidence for any claim intended for packaging or marketing, and have the importer confirm destination-market wording. Do not infer compostability or recyclability from appearance.

### Build a measurable material specification

Include these fields in the RFQ and approved specification:

- Paper grade or construction, colour and finish.
- Nominal basis weight with the agreed test method and tolerance.
- Caliper or stiffness requirement only where the application needs it.
- Grease-resistance acceptance method based on the real food or an agreed laboratory method.
- Wet or dry strength checks relevant to folding, pickup and delivery.
- Odour and visible cleanliness requirements.
- Substance, coating or treatment disclosures needed by the buyer’s compliance team.
- Intended food, contact time, temperature, storage and reheating conditions.
- Sustainability claims permitted on the pack, with supporting evidence and market review.

## 3. Size the Sheet Around the Wrap or Liner Pattern

Sheet dimensions should come from a physical packing trial. For a wrap, measure enough paper to cover the product and retain the agreed overlap after staff make their normal folds. For a tray liner, decide whether the edges should lie flat, turn up the sides or remain visible as part of the presentation. For an interleaf, check product coverage and removal without adding unnecessary material.

Specify width and length in millimetres, dimensional tolerances, squareness and cut-edge quality. If the sheet has a preferred print direction or grain direction, show both on the drawing. A square sheet with directional artwork still needs an orientation rule. For pre-cut sheets, define pieces per inner pack, packs per case and whether sheets are loose, banded, bagged, interfolded or dispensed from a carton.

Trial the sheet at the work station. Observe how quickly staff can pick one sheet, whether two sheets cling together, whether corners curl, and whether a full stack remains aligned. For automated feeding or converting, run enough material at operating speed to expose double feeds, skew, dust, curl or static problems. One hand-folded sample cannot approve a production process.

## 4. Treat Custom Printing as a Food-Packaging Component

Custom print can turn a functional liner into a branded touchpoint, but it also adds variables: ink system, coverage, colour reference, registration, set-off, rub resistance and potential odour. Give the supplier a revision-controlled artwork file and identify the food-contact side. If the design repeats, define repeat length, orientation and acceptable cutoff position so logos do not appear randomly cropped at key folds.

Ask for the ink and printing-process information your compliance reviewer needs for the intended market and use. The FDA notes that the regulatory status of a food-contact material is determined by its component substances and their applicable specifications and limitations. A generic statement about “food-safe ink” is not a substitute for identifying the finished article, intended conditions and compliance basis. The FDA’s [component-status guidance](https://www.fda.gov/food/packaging-food-contact-substances-fcs/determining-regulatory-status-components-food-contact-material) explains this component-by-component approach.

Approve colour under defined lighting against a physical standard or controlled proof. Test production-style sheets with the actual food for transfer, blocking, scuffing, odour and appearance. ## 5. Review Food-Contact Evidence Against the Finished Article

Food-contact review is not a logo-collection exercise. Connect the paper, treatment or coating, ink and finished sheet to its intended use and market. Reports should identify the sample, construction, regulatory basis, test conditions, results, laboratory and date. If evidence covers unprinted paper but the order is printed, ask how the finished article is supported.

For the EU, Regulation (EC) No 1935/2004 provides the general framework for materials intended to contact food. The European Commission states that food-contact materials must not release constituents at levels harmful to health or change food composition, taste or odour in an unacceptable way. It also explains that all food-contact materials must be produced under good manufacturing practice, while paper and board may also be subject to Member State requirements because there is no single fully harmonised EU measure for paper and board. Buyers can review the Commission’s current [food-contact materials overview](https://food.ec.europa.eu/food-safety/chemical-safety/food-contact-materials_en) and [legislation summary](https://food.ec.europa.eu/food-safety/chemical-safety/food-contact-materials/legislation_en).

For U.S. sales, ask for the compliance basis of each relevant component and confirm its restrictions and conditions of use. FDA guidance points buyers to applicable regulations in 21 CFR, effective Food Contact Substance Notifications and other authorization routes. An FCN can be manufacturer-specific, so a reference number should be matched to the actual substance source rather than copied from an unrelated document.

A BfR recommendation may be useful technical evidence for some European specifications, but it is not the same as an EU-wide product approval. BfR’s [Recommendation XXXVI for paper and board for food contact](https://www.bfr.bund.de/cm/349/XXXVI-Paper-and-Board-for-Food-Contact.pdf) should be read with its scope, temperature limits and intended applications. The importer’s compliance team remains responsible for deciding what evidence is required in the destination market.

## 6. Run a Representative Sample Approval

Approve three layers: a material sample, a printed or converted pre-production sample, and production-representative pieces where order risk justifies them. Label retained samples with SKU, revision, supplier, date and approval conditions. Keep enough sheets for comparison when bulk goods arrive.

### Practical application test

1. Condition the test sheets and control sample in the same room and keep them protected from odours.
2. Prepare the highest-risk food within the intended menu range, including its normal oil, sauce and serving temperature.
3. Use the actual wrap, liner or interleaf method and the planned contact time, including delivery hold where relevant.
4. Inspect for grease strike-through, moisture softening, tears, sticking, ink transfer, odour and unacceptable appearance.
5. Compare results with the retained approved sample and record photos, time, temperature and food description.
6. Repeat across several sheets and more than one inner pack; do not approve from a single best-looking piece.

Application testing does not replace regulatory review or required laboratory testing. Agree the laboratory, sample identity, protocol and acceptance criterion before testing. ## 7. Design Packaging for Cleanliness and Flatness

Paper sheets can arrive technically compliant but commercially unusable if they absorb warehouse odours, curl, crease or become contaminated. Define the inner protection, stack quantity, case count, carton dimensions, carton strength, moisture protection, labels and pallet pattern. Packaging should keep sheets flat and clean through export handling without compressing them so tightly that staff cannot separate them.

For foodservice packs, verify a practical station quantity and whether the inner wrap can be reclosed. For retail packs, check artwork revision, count, barcode, language, importer details and market-specific material statements. Scan production barcodes and case labels.

Ask for a packed sample before locking the case. Measure the real stack because paper thickness, air between sheets, bands and bags affect carton height. Compare landed cost per usable sheet, not only price per kilogram or per thousand. A low unit price can be offset by excess pack volume, damaged corners, inaccurate counts or slow dispensing.

## 8. Inspect Bulk Goods Against the Approved Specification

The purchase order should reference the current drawing, material specification, artwork revision, retained sample, pack specification, test requirements and destination-market documents. Agree a risk-based sampling plan and defect classifications before production. There is no responsible fixed AQL that fits every product, customer and failure consequence.

### Pre-shipment inspection checklist

- Confirm SKU, purchase order, lot identification and production quantity.
- Pull cartons from different pallet positions and production lots.
- Measure sheet width, length, squareness and basis weight using agreed methods.
- Compare paper colour, finish, print colour, repeat, registration and cutoff with approved references.
- Check count, stack alignment, curl, edge damage, dust, foreign matter and odour.
- Repeat the agreed grease or application test on sampled production sheets.
- Verify inner protection, case count, carton marks, barcode and pallet pattern.
- Review test reports, declarations and certificates against product identity, scope and validity.
- Record defects by type and lot; hold affected goods until disposition is agreed.
- Retain labelled samples and inspection evidence for repeat orders or claims.

If bulk sheets differ from the sample, do not solve the problem by changing the specification after the fact. Quarantine the affected lots, compare measurements and application results, and investigate paper supply, moisture, printing, cutting, packing compression and storage. Decide whether sorting, rework, concession or replacement is appropriate based on risk and the written purchase agreement.

## 9. Send an RFQ Suppliers Can Quote Consistently

A clean RFQ prevents suppliers from filling gaps with different assumptions. Send the same brief to each bidder and require stated deviations. Request the unit-price basis, tooling or plate charges, sample route, schedule assumptions, case quantities, pallet data and quote validity. Tie timing to artwork and sample approval, material availability and production capacity.

Your RFQ should include:

- Application, food type, contact temperature, duration and storage conditions.
- Sheet dimensions, tolerance, grain and print orientation.
- Paper grade, basis weight, colour, finish and barrier requirement.
- Print process, colours, coverage, food-contact side and artwork revision.
- Required application tests and laboratory evidence.
- Destination markets and compliance documents for review.
- Pieces per inner pack, packs per case, labels, pallet and shipping method.
- Forecast volume, order quantity, sample quantity and approval milestones.
- Inspection plan, defect definitions and retained-sample requirements.

Compare proposals only after normalizing these fields. A supplier offering a different paper construction, pack count or compliance scope is not providing a directly comparable price.

## FAQ

### What information should I send for a wholesale greaseproof paper sheet quote?

Send the food application, contact time and temperature, sheet size, paper preference, grease-resistance requirement, print file, pack count, destination market, order volume and required documents. Include photos or a physical reference if the sheet must duplicate an existing wrap or liner.

### Is greaseproof paper the same as wax paper or parchment paper?

No. These names can describe different constructions and intended uses, and terminology varies by market. Require the supplier to identify the offered material, barrier mechanism and supported conditions of use instead of approving by a familiar name.

### Can one greaseproof sheet work for burgers, fries and pastries?

Possibly, but it should not be assumed. The hottest, oiliest and wettest application may need stronger barrier and handling performance than a dry pastry. Test each intended food and choose either one validated specification or separate SKUs.

### How should buyers test grease resistance?

Use an agreed method tied to the intended food and holding time. A practical trial should record food, temperature, contact time, folding method and visible strike-through, then compare several sheets with a retained control. Use laboratory testing when a regulatory, claim or customer requirement calls for it.

### Does an FDA certificate prove a printed sheet is compliant?

FDA does not issue a universal finished-product certificate for every paper sheet. Buyers should review the regulatory basis of relevant components, the source of any manufacturer-specific authorization, and the conditions of use, then connect that evidence to the finished printed article.

### What should be checked on custom printed sheets?

Check artwork revision, orientation, print repeat, colour, registration, cutoff, rub resistance, set-off, odour and visible transfer during the real food test. Confirm that documentation covers the ink system and finished construction where required.

### How should wholesale sheets be packed for export?

Use clean inner protection and a carton and pallet plan that keeps stacks flat, dry, odour-free and count-controlled. Approve a packed sample, verify carton marks and barcodes, and inspect cartons from multiple pallet positions before shipment.

## Conclusion

Wholesale greaseproof paper buying is a specification exercise. Start with the food, service process and market; then define construction, dimensions, print, packaging and evidence. Approve the paper using representative food and production-style packing, preserve a labelled reference, and inspect the shipment against the same revision-controlled requirements.

This sequence makes supplier comparisons meaningful and reduces disputes about grease performance, print, counts, curl, odour and document scope.
